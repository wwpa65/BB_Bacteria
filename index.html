Bateria Found in the Bellybutton Among Volunteers

Human body is known to contain more number of microbes (most of them are bacteria) in the body than the number of the cells the body is composed of. Many parts of the body can be the habitats to the bacterial communities. Among them, belly button considered a habitat to bateria closest to us (1). Scientists have found that the bacterial communities are highly diverse in composition of the bacterial taxonomy and the number of bacteria belonging to the taxa (1, 2).

This project involves the display of information of demographics and microbial composition of belly buttons of volunteers acquired from volunteers in the form of a dashboard. Microbial Operational Taxonomic Units (OTUs), number, and the washing frequeny of belly button was included in the analysis. The bar plot shows the top 10 bacterial OTUs found in the individuals. Bubble plot shows the OTUs, number of bacteria in each OTU (colored by the OTU and the size based on the number of bacteria). When a Sample ID is selected from the dropdown menu, the bashboard displays information on that spectific sample (individual). As you see, there is a diverse communities of bacteria are found among individuals participated in the project.
